# Ruined Wold
*by Sebastian Huschina*

> "You are stranded in an unknown world, you cannot remember anything. So your only option is to explore this world and find out what is going on."

---

## 1. First Location

You have awoken in a **Forest Clearing**. Open the Landmark Atlas and find Landmark 1 (Page 1).

> "You do not know where you are and without rations or shelter your only option is to Explore the surrounding area."

---

## 2. Exploring

When **Exploring** you have to face **Challenges** depending on the path you take.

In the Landmark Atlas you see your only option is exploring the surrounding area, which is part of **Zone Deck One [ZD1]** — shuffle it and place it in front of you.

When Exploring you reveal Challenge Cards from the Zone Deck one by one; after each reveal you can stop or reveal another.

All revealed Challenges must be overcome in one turn, so revealing too much can be fatal.

> "You feel an urge to Explore but also do not want to go too far on your first Exploration. So you Reveal 2 Challenges..."

**Each Challenge you overcome:**

1. Choose an **Approach** to determine the Difficulty.
2. If you use a **Tool**, subtract its **Power** from this Approach’s Difficulty (section 5). Suffer **Exhaustion** equal to what’s left (not below 0), plus any **Exhaust** printed on that line.
3. Resolve **Outcomes** (Details in section 11).
4. Discard the Challenge to the Zone Deck discard pile.

*For Example: when you choose to Traverse around the Wolf, you suffer 2 Exhaustion and gain 1 Progress and track both.*

*Playtesting:* Reveal **2–3** Challenges while learning tools so **Area** and **Speed** matter. For Landmark 1, **12–14 Required Progress** on the first path is a good target. Runs stay interesting when **Progress**, **Recover**, **Rations**, and **Maintain** (5 Exhaustion — sections 5–6) all pull you different ways; a tight Stop is often **8–14 Exhaustion** before **Rest** or **Maintain**; **15** still means abandon. A **printable outcome summary** for the table is in **cards.html** (filter **Reference**).*

---

## 3. Challenges

Challenges are defined by the available **Approaches** to overcome them and the corresponding **Difficulties** and **Outcomes** for each Approach.

Approaches can be: **Fight**, **Work**, **Traverse**, or **Awareness**.

In addition to that, many Challenges also come with **Special Effects**.

The Wolf, for example, can be Overcome with the **Fight** or **Traverse** Approach — where you have to decide: do you want the better Outcome, or the smaller Difficulty?

> "As you see the two Challenges your mind automatically tries to find ways to overcome them..."

**To Overcome a Challenge, go through these steps in reveal order:**

| Part | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Approach** | The method you use (Fight, Work, Traverse, Awareness) |
| **Difficulty** | The Exhaustion cost you must suffer |
| **Outcome** | What you gain (Progress, Findings, Recovery…) |
| **Special Effect** | Additional rules that modify the Challenge |

---

## 4. Exhaustion Limit

The normal **Exhaustion Limit is 15**. When your Exhaustion reaches the Limit or above you must abandon your Exploration and go back to the Landmark you started Exploring from.

> "As you think about the consequences of failing, you see something hanging in a tree. As you get closer you recognize a Backpack filled with a few tools. You take out the Tools (Cards) and place them in front of you."

---

## 5. Tools

Tools can be used for one or more Approaches and have corresponding **Power** and **Effects** for each Approach.

Tool Effects are described on the card or use Keywords (see section 12).

### Equipping Tools

Your **Backpack** can hold many Tools but you can only **equip 4** of them. Only Equipped Tools can be actively used.

### Using Tools

When choosing an Approach to Overcome a Challenge, you may also choose to use **one Tool** that can be used for that Approach. 

When using a Tool, you **reduce the Approach Difficulty by the Tool Power** and resolve Tool Effects.

A Tool may list several lines with the same or a different Approach. When using the tool you have to choose which you want to use .

*For Example: when the **War Blade** row (Power 7) is used to Fight the Wolf (Difficulty 6), you would suffer 6 − 7 = 0 Exhaustion.*

After a Tool Card is used it is **discarded**.

<div class="rule-box">
<p>You can use at most <strong>one Tool</strong> for all <strong>currently revealed</strong> Challenges. Use another Tool only after <strong>every</strong> revealed Challenge in that set has been Overcome.</p>
</div>

Many Tools have Effects that help with overcoming many Challenges at once, like **Area**, **Speed**, and **Range** (see section 12 for details).

### Maintaining Tools

When **Maintaining Tools**, you regain all discarded Tools and may swap Equipped Tools with Tools from your Backpack.

- At a **Landmark** you can Maintain Tools for **free**.
- During **Exploration** you may Maintain Tools once all revealed Challenges have been Overcome (see section 6) by suffering **5 Exhaustion**.

> "After you overcome the second challenge you decide to pause and go over your options."

---

## 6. Stops

A **Stop** occurs each time you overcome all revealed Challenges.

Between Stops, you may use **at most one Tool** when overcoming Challenges (see section 5).

When you reach a Stop you have the following choices:

- **Maintain Tools** (regain and swap) by suffering 5 Exhaustion.
- **Abandon** your Exploration and return to your starting Landmark.
- **Continue Exploring** by doing another turn of revealing and overcoming Challenges.
- **Choose a Path** to another Landmark.
- **Travel** to another Landmark when you have the Required Progress.

> "You have not yet found anything, so your options are either going back to the Forest Clearing or continuing Exploring. After a while you decide..."
>
> *Abandon your Exploration → [Read section 7. Landmarks]*
> *Continue Exploration → [Continue Exploring as before]*

---

## 7. Landmarks

When you reach a **Landmark** all Progress resets to 0 and you resolve all **arrival effects** for the Landmark.

Afterwards you have the following options:

1. **Maintain Tools** (regain and swap) for free.
2. **Rest**.
3. **Choose a Path** to Travel to other Landmarks.
4. **Additional Options** specific to the Landmark.

> "Now that you have reached a Landmark again, you should Rest before you decide what path to take next."

---

## 8. Rest

When you **Rest** you recover from Exhaustion. How much you can recover depends on many factors:

- The Landmark you are at.
- Whether you have **Rations** or **Shelter**.

**Rations** (from outcomes such as **Rations X**) are supplies you **track**, not immediate healing. When you **Rest**, resolve the Landmark’s normal Rest first; then you may **spend** any number of Rations, and **each Ration spent removes 1 Exhaustion** (unless a Landmark or card says otherwise).

The **Base Rest Value** is defined by the Landmark; modifiers are found on Cards.

> "After Resting it is time to Explore again and hopefully Discover something."

---

## 9. Findings and Discoveries

To **Discover** something you need first to **Find** something on a Challenge Card.

- **Findings** are Outcomes for specific Approaches when Overcoming Challenges.
- A Finding leads you to a **Discovery** on the current Landmark Page.

*For Example: on one Landmark page, Finding **F2** might point to Discovery **D7** in the Discovery Book — the atlas defines the link for each Landmark.*

A working **F → D** key list for design lives in **discoveries.md** (draft). All Discoveries are noted on the current Landmark Page so that you can use this knowledge for future Explorations.

---

## 10. Choose a Path

When at a **Landmark** or a **Stop** you can Choose a Path to any Landmark you can reach from the current Landmark. In both cases you **set your Progress to 0**.

When you Choose a Path, note the **Required Progress** for your Chosen Path.

Then take out the corresponding **Zone Deck** and shuffle it. You do this even when the Deck was used before.

*For Example: Path "Smoke over the Trees [7P-ZD2] → L2" means:*
- *The Required Progress is 7.*
- *Take out Zone Deck 2 and shuffle it.*
- *This Path leads to Landmark 2.*

When you do an **Area Exploration**, you do not have Required Progress but you will still gain Progress. When you then Choose a Path at a Stop, you can use this Progress for the newly Set Path.

> "After some thinking you have decided to follow the newly Discovered Path, and see what awaits you at the end of it."

---

## 11. Travel

When you have Chosen a Path to another Landmark, reached the Required Progress and are at a Stop, you may **Travel** to that Landmark.

To do so, open the **Landmark Atlas Page** for that Landmark. When you arrive don't forget to reset your Progress to zero and Resolve arrival Effects.

## 12. Effect Activations

The following Keywords are combined with Effects to define their Activation.

*Listed alphabetically for quick lookup.*

<!-- AUTO:SYNC:activation-keywords -->

| Keyword | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **`[X+]`** | When X or more Exhaustion is suffered on the related line, the Effect activates (see bracket notation on cards). |
| **Approach** | Effect only activates when you choose that specific Approach (Fight, Work, Traverse, Awareness). |
| **Charging** | Only activates after the first player Action, after revealing Challenges. |
| **Close** | Can only activate for the first Challenge in Reveal Order. |
| **Grouping** | Only activates when there is at least one other revealed card with the same Name; name specific versions on cards as needed (e.g. Grouping [Wolf] in prose). |
| **Last Challenge** | Only activates when there is only one revealed Challenge left. |
| **On Reveal** | Activates only once, when this Challenge is revealed. |
| **Ongoing** | Active as long as the Challenge is revealed. |
| **On Use** | Activates when a Tool card is used. |
| **Ranged** | Can only activate for the secound Challenge in Reveal Order or higher. |

<!-- /AUTO:SYNC:activation-keywords -->

## 13. Universal Effects

*Listed alphabetically for quick lookup.*

<!-- AUTO:SYNC:universal-effects -->

| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Exhaust X** | Gain X Exhaustion for your Character. |
| **Find FX** | You find a Discovery (see rules §9). |
| **Progress X** | Gain X Progress, required for Travel. |
| **Rations X** | Gain X **Rations** and track them on your character. They are supplies, not immediate healing — you **spend** them when you **Rest** (see §8), unless a card says you may spend them elsewhere. |
| **Recover X** | Remove X Exhaustion from your Character **immediately** when the outcome resolves. |
